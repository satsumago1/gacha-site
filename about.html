What is gacha?

Gacha, short for gachapon or gashapon, is a capsule dispensing vending machine originating from Japan. Traditionally, people insert coins inside the machines to get a capsule. Inside the capsule is a small toy based on the selected machine.

Since the 1960s, gachapon has exploded in popularity, evolving into various subtypes like gacha video games, blind boxes, bottle cap figures, and more.

Sounds fun! And addicting!

Right you are!
However, gacha bears close similarities to gambling. As both play with people’s sense of luck and satisfaction, it can lead to financially poor decisions.

This video shows the extent to which people are willing to pay to get the character they want.

Since 2016, the Computer Entertainment Supplier’s Association (CESA) passed a law requiring companies show the probability of drawing items in money-involving gacha. This allows players to properly understand their chances of winning. For all the reasons gacha is fun it can be dangerous as well, so it’s important to consider one’s mental state and wallet before playing.
